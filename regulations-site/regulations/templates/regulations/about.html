About eRegulations

{% block about-intro %}

eRegulations makes regulations easier to read and navigate. It clarifies regulations by bringing related information and regulatory history to the forefront. It is a work in progress by the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau, and is a public domain work of the United States Government.

Github

Developers

eRegulations is an open source project. Visit the project repository to learn more and contribute.

Easier to read

Clear typography makes regulations legible and easy to scan.

Responsive design adapts the tool to fit to your screen.

Easy to navigate

Persistent table of contents for fast access to any section of the regulation.

  • Organized by subpart and section of the regulation
  • Section you are in stays highlighted at all times
  • Can be collapsed to increase space for the regulation text

Regulation timeline shows recent revisions of the regulation organized by effective date.

{% endblock %} {% block about-related-info %}

Related information

  1. Defined terms are highlighted in the text and displayed in the right sidebar so you can keep your place.
  2. References to other parts of the regulation are linked.
  3. Official interpretations from Supplement I are brought inline with the regulation for easy access and comparisons.
  4. Section-by-section analysis from the preamble of Federal Register notices is available directly in the tool.
    • Read in-depth analysis of changes to the regulation
    • Understand the reasoning behind those changes
    Limited section-by-section available. Check the Federal Register for more.
{% endblock %} {% block about-timeline %}

Regulation timeline

The regulation timeline shows recent revisions of the regulation organized by latest effective date.

  1. The date picker allows users to find what revision was effective on a specific date.
  2. Federal Register notice(s) associated with a revision are presented and organized by publication date. These links will take you to the notice on the Office of the Federal Register’s website.
  3. Compare any two revisions of the regulation, word for word, by selecting a date in the menu.
